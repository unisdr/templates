Disaster Loss Datasets

This page aims at providing valuable disaster loss information by facilitating centralized access to disaster loss databases worldwide.

EM-DAT

CRED, Universit√© de Louvain

EM-DAT contains data on the occurrence and impact of over 16,000 disasters in the world from 1900 to present. Disasters are broken down into two major groups: natural and technological (man-made). For each disaster the following information is provided: dates, country, number of people killed, injured, homeless and affected, as well as estimates of infrastructure and economic damages.

Disaster database project

University of Richmond

1552 recorded entries, the earliest dating to a volcanic eruption in Java in 2000BCE. Disasters are classified into natural disasters, conflict-based disasters, and human systems failures. The database collects information on location, date and time, class and intensity of event. Along with people missing, injured, and displaced the database attempts to collect information on animals killed, buildings destroyed, the area covered, and the cost of the disaster.

Global Disaster Identifier (GLIDE) Database

ADRC

The Global Disaster Identifier Number (GLIDE) is a project initiated and maintained by the Asian Disaster Reduction Center (ADRC) in collaboration with ISDR, CRED, UNDP, IFRC, FAO, World Bank, OFDA/USAID, LA Red, and OCHA/ReliefWeb. A GLIDE number is generated for all disaster events. The number is then attached to all databases documenting the same disaster, thereby linking the various information sources. The GLIDE database is searchable by date, disaster type, country, and GLIDE number.
